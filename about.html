Phi Beta Sigma Fraternity Inc.

We were founded on January 9th, 1914 on the campus of Howard University by three extraordinary men. A. L. Taylor, L. F. Morse, and Charles I. Brown, who saw it fit to create an organization that embodied the ideals of brotherhood, scholarship, and service. Their undertaking has survived for over 104 years and continues to grow to this day.

We continue to govern ourselves by our motto "Culture for service, service for humanity."

Naughty Nu Beta Chapter

Chartered on May 11th, 1981 the Nu Beta Chapter has strived to continuously impact the Georgia Institute of Technology through Brotherhood, Scholarship, and Service. We are a testament of their continued legacy. Charterd by 10 honorable men the Nu Beta chapter still stands after 36 years.

As an organization we center ourselves on serving our community and making a profound positive influence on it's members. Many notable alumni have passed through our chapter and have go on to accomplish fantastic things. While some current brothers are beginning to carve out their own legacy.

Our Sisters

Glamarous Gamma Rho Chapter

We are the only NPHC fraternity to be constituitionally bound to a sorority. Founded on January 16, 1920 by five extraordinary women Arizon L. Cleaver Stemons, Myrtle T. Faithful, Viola T. Goinngs, Pearl A. Neal, and Fannie P. Watts on the campus of Howard University. The Glamarous Gamma Rho Chapter of Zeta Phi Beta Sorority Inc. was chartered on February 12, 2000 and has continued to express the principles of Scholarship, Service, Sisterhood, and Finer Womanhood on the Georgia Tech campus. We share a special bond to say the least and often times you will see us collaborating on events on campus.
